Layoffs
Microsoft
In January 2025, Microsoft announced layoffs affecting less than 1% of its global workforce, targeting underperforming employees across various departments, including the security division. This move is part of the company's focus on maintaining high-performance talent and managing costs. Additionally, Microsoft has implemented a hiring freeze in its U.S. consulting unit to better control expenses and prioritize investments in artificial intelligence.
Google
In December 2024, Google laid off 10% of its management positions as part of a strategy to increase efficiency and reduce roles. This decision was announced during an all-hands meeting by CEO Sundar Pichai, aiming to make the company 20% more efficient amid rising competition in the AI industry. Furthermore, in February 2025, Google introduced a "voluntary exit program" for its U.S. Platforms and Devices team, offering severance packages to employees willing to leave. This initiative follows earlier layoffs and restructuring efforts to streamline operations and enhance efficiency. ​
Atlassian
As of April 2025, there are no publicly reported layoffs at Atlassian. The company continues to offer job opportunities in various locations, including India.
Hackathon

Google Summer of Code is an annual, global program that offers students and beginners in open source a unique opportunity to gain real-world experience by working on 12+ week coding projects with open-source organizations. The program aims to introduce new contributors to open-source software development under the guidance of experienced mentors

Adobe has been involved in various hackathons, such as the Adobe Analytics Challenge, which offers university students the opportunity to use Adobe's industry-leading analytics products and access real-world data from leading organizations.
